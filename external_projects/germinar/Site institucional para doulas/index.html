What comes up

Seeds with stories

Every variety is an heirloom with a past — the lettuce a lighthouse keeper bred for wind, the tomato that crossed an ocean in a coat pocket. The card in the box tells you who kept it alive and how to return the favor.

Planted, not planned

You don’t choose the seeds — the season does. We pack what wants to go in the ground the week the box lands, so the garden stays a small, regular surprise instead of a cart to abandon.

Help when it wilts

Something will wilt. Write to us — a person who gardens reads every letter and answers with what to try next, not a link to a help center. Most things come back. The rest become compost and wisdom.

Grown here

Pictures that sit back in the bed

Every photograph is washed — softened and lifted until it settles into the cream page like it grew there. The garden is the loud part; the pictures keep their voices down.
